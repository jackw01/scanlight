## big scanlight assembly instructions

### 1

Remove the protective film from the acrylic sheet and insert it into the diffuser bezel.

![](<images/01.png>)

### 2

With the diffuser bezel sitting face down on a flat surface, engage the rim of the diffuser box (on the side with the two protruding flanges) with the hooks on the matching side of the diffuser bezel. Fit the alignment posts on the bezel into the matching holes on the rim of the diffuser box.

![](<images/02.png>)

![](<images/03.png>)

Press the diffuser box onto the bezel to fasten it in place, applying pressure straight downwards in the locations and order shown.

![](<images/04.png>)

### 3

Insert the buttons into the rear side of the PCB cover. Snap the driver PCB into the cover.

![](<images/05.png>)

![](<images/06.png>)

### 4

Insert the PCB assembly into the recess on the side of the diffuser box, aligning the mounting holes on the PCB with the mounting bosses on the diffuser box. Secure the PCB and cover in place with two M2x10 self-tapping screws using a T6 Torx screwdriver.

![](<images/07.png>)

![](<images/08.png>)

### 5

Use compressed air or a handheld blower tool to remove any dust and debris from the inside of the diffuser box. Place the LED PCB into the opening on the rear of the diffuser box, aligning the black connector on the LED PCB with the pins on the driver PCB. Press down on the back of the LED PCB around the connector area to connect the PCBs together.

![](<images/09.png>)

![](<images/10.png>)

### 6

Place the backplate over the PCB so that the protruding flange covers the exposed edge of the LED PCB, lining up the two rectangular holes at the edge of the backplate with the tabs protruding from the driver PCB cover. Press on the backplate around these holes to snap the backplate onto the PCB cover. Install the four M3x8 self-tapping screws that secure the LED PCB and backplate to the diffuser box using a T9 Torx screwdriver.

![](<images/11.png>)

![](<images/12.png>)

### 7

Clean the circular areas on the backplate where the rubber feet will attach with a paper towel and isopropanol. Stick on the rubber feet.

![](<images/13.png>)
